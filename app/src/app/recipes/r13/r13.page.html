Ginisang Ampalaya The thought we often associate with “ampalaya” is bitterness and a generally unpleasant taste. And while it is true that this vegetable holds this flavor— after all, it is called a bitter melon— it doesn’t always have to taste unfavorable. with the right preparations, it can actually taste satisfyingly flavorful and delicious. A classic dish that utilizes this unique flavor is Ginisang Ampalaya. Nutrition: per serving kcal: 152 fat: 13g saturates: 1g carbs: 6g sugar: 2g fiber: 2g protein: 4g salt: 0.04g Ingredients:
  • 2 pieces ampalaya cleaned and cut into thin slices
  • 1 tbsp garlic minced
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • salt to taste
  • 2 raw eggs
  • 18 ounces luke warm water
  • 1 tomato sliced
  • 1 piece tomato cubed
  • 1 onion sliced
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
    •  Instructions Step 1 Place the ampalaya in a large bowl Add salt and lukewarm water then leave for 5 minutes Place the ampalaya in a cheesecloth then squeeze tightly until all liquid drips Step 2 Heat the pan and place the cooking oil Saute the garlic, onion, and tomato Add the ampalaya mix well with the other ingredients Step 3 Put-in salt and pepper to taste Beat the eggs and pour over the ampalaya then let the eggs cook partially Mix the egg with the other ingredients Serve hot.
